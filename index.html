a little ABOUT us

HoneyBrew is dedicated to pairing what naturally goes together - honey and tea.

We thrive on the belief that what tastes good can also be good for you and good for the planet, and that small indulgences should be enjoyed daily.

We create a simple, beautiful array of the highest quality products and experiences, all made with pride and care, responsibly, sustainably and fairly.

fair TRADE tea

Herbal Tea

Tea made from the infusion or decoction of herbs, spices or other plant material in hot water.

Chai Tea

A tea made using different formulas, depending on the region where it is being consumed.

White Tea

A tea of the most delicate type; they are not oxidized.

Oolong Tea

A tea often compared to the taste and aroma of fresh flowers or fresh fruit, they undergo partial oxidation.

Green Tea

A tea that is pale in color and slightly bitter in flavor; they are not oxidized.

Black Tea

A tea of the most usual type; they usually undergo full oxidation.

small BATCH honey

Origional Honey

A light amber honey from numerous species of late summer blooming Aster flowers. Very herbal with notes of Thyme and Eucalyptus.

Blueberry Honey

Taken from the tiny white flowers of the blueberry bush, the nectar makes a honey which is typically light amber or amber in color and with a full, well-rounded flavor. Pure Blueberry honey is the result of bees gathering nectar from the Blueberry bush.

Sage Honey

Sage honey can come from many different species of the sage plant. Sage honey has a mild, delicate flavor. It is generally white or water- white in color. It is quite sweet in flavor, and pairs extremely well with strong cheeses.

Orange Blossom Honey

Orange Blossom honey is a combination of citrus floral sources from Oranges and nearby Grapefruit or even Lime and Lemon trees. Orange trees produce a white to extra light amber honey with a distinctive flavor and the aroma of orange blossoms.

Sourwood Honey

Despite its name, the Sourwood tree has a sweet, spicy, anise aroma and flavor. The honey has been highly valued for table use or in a myriad of cooking applications such as glazes, and can have a lingering aftertaste.

Buckwheat Honey

Buckwheat plants grow best in cool, moist climates. It blooms quite early and it yields a dark brown honey of strong, distinct flavor. Buckwheat has excellent application for sauces and baked goods.
